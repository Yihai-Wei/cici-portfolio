About Me

From an early age, I discovered my voice not merely as an instrument of sound, but as a vessel of emotion. Trained in the classical tradition of Bel canto, I have dedicated myself to exploring the depth, nuance, and expressiveness that this art form embodies. Through years of vocal study, stage performance, and collaboration with pianists and chamber ensembles, I have come to appreciate how music bridges the gap between discipline and imagination. Whether interpreting the works of Mozart and Donizetti or experimenting with contemporary compositions, I strive to communicate sincerity, balance, and beauty in every phrase. Beyond performance, I am deeply interested in the intersection of voice pedagogy and artistic innovation — how centuries-old technique can coexist with modern interpretation. As I continue my journey in graduate study, my goal is to refine my artistry, expand my repertoire, and contribute my voice to the evolving landscape of classical music.
